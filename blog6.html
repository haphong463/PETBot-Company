Introducing Prevented Ocean Plastic to our range

By PETBot's Staff, 16 Nov 2022.

We've all read about the effects of ocean plastic pollution on marine mammals and species living in our oceans. Scenes of floating plastic alongside dolphins and seals are a challenging visual to shake once seen.

There have been many initiatives to collect this plastic from the ocean surface but much of the plastic retrieved has been degraded by the sun and is unsuitable for use as recycled plastic material. Focus has therefore switched to reducing plastic debris from reaching the ocean in the first place. This is done by removing it from waterways and coastlines when it is still a usable source of plastic for recycling.

As you know The Plastic Bottles Company is committed to operating as sustainably as we can and encouraging others to do the same. Our sustainable and recycled range is growing every month and we’re delighted to announce that we are now introducing products which include Prevented Ocean Plastic (POP).

What is Prevented Ocean Plastic?

There are strict regulations and qualifying criteria for plastic to be defined as Prevented Ocean Plastic.

Experts define it as:

  • Plastics found within 30 miles (50km) of an ocean coastline or major waterway that feeds into the ocean
  • The country must lack a viable waste management system and collection incentives
  • The infrastructure is being overwhelmed by tourism or population growth
  • There is a significant risk to wildlife if plastic contaminates their ecosystem

Each ton of plastic waste is certified by OceanCycle, the leader in third party audits for ocean bound plastics, ensuring international quality, environmental, ethical and labour standards are met. They also check that plastic has only been collected from at-risk areas such as South East Asia, South America and the Galapagos Islands, the Mediterranean and Central America & Caribbean.

The Prevented Ocean Plastic programme has been in operation since 2005 and has so far prevented 10 billion bottles from entering the ocean. It continues to prevent 1,000 tonnes of plastic from entering the ocean every month and offers brand owners and customers the ability to clearly track materials in their products back to the coastal communities affected.

Prevented Ocean Plastic at The Plastic Bottles Company

We believe this programme further highlights our commitment to using plastic in a responsible way and provides opportunities for our customers to join us in operating sustainably.

By joining the programme, and being licensed to use the Prevented Ocean Plastic Trademarks on our qualified products we agree:

  • To a minimum POP content of 30%
  • To demonstrate a larger scale commitment with minimum order quantities agreed
  • To maintain the traceability of the programme
  • To communicate and support the legitimacy and wider vision of the programme.
